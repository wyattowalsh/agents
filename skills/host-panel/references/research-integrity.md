# Research Integrity

- Use 3-5 relevant sources when possible.
- Prefer academic papers, established analyses, real debates between named scholars, and literature reviews.
- If search is unavailable or thin, say: "Based on training knowledge - not verified against current literature."
- Cite specific works only when confident; otherwise cite the tradition or framework instead.
- Never fabricate titles, authors, years, journal names, or affiliations.
- If the topic has a live debate, identify actual participants and positions.
- Strong evidence is better than a fake citation. If in doubt, stay generic and truthful.
