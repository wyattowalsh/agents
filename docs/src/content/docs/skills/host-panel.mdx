## What It Does

Surface real tensions, frameworks, and disagreements through simulated expert discourse -- not theatrical roleplay. The panel explores a complex topic from multiple angles, surfacing frameworks and genuine disagreements rather than producing consensus or truth.

<Aside type="note" title="Current contract">
The current source routes every request through topic diagnosis first. Weak, settled, or under-researched topics can be reframed or paused before personas are created.
</Aside>

## Discussion Formats

| Format | Purpose | Best for |
|--------|---------|----------|
| `roundtable` | Open multi-perspectival exploration | Broad topics, brainstorming, mapping a field |
| `oxford` | Binary debate with formal sides | Policy decisions, testing propositions |
| `socratic` | Deep inquiry through questioning | Conceptual analysis, definitional disputes |

**Defaults:** roundtable format, 4 experts. Expert range: 2-6 (prefer 4-5 for best persona maintenance).

<Aside type="tip" title="Format auto-selection">
If the user omits the format, the skill auto-selects: binary propositions ("Should X...") get oxford, open explorations ("What are the implications of...") get roundtable, and deep conceptual inquiries ("What does X mean?") get socratic.
</Aside>

## Panel Pipeline

<Steps>

1. **Argument Parsing and Topic Diagnostic** -- Parse the quoted topic, optional format keyword, and optional expert count (2-6). Evaluate topic suitability: settled science gets reframed, too-broad topics get narrowed, too-narrow topics get expanded.

2. **Topic Analysis and Research Grounding** -- Map the terrain: identify core disciplines, key tensions, intellectual traditions with substantive positions, and what is specifically contested. Use WebSearch for 3-5 recent sources. Output: topic map, research brief, and suggested panel composition.

3. **Persona Generation** -- Build personas that maximally cover identified tensions. Each panelist gets: name/credentials, specific domain expertise, intellectual tradition (operationalized), argumentative style, and known blind spots. Diversity requirements: no two from the same tradition, at least one contrarian, at least one bridge figure, mixed career stages.

4. **Discussion Phases** -- Phase 0 (Framing) is universal. Format-specific phases are loaded from `references/formats.md`. Moderator standing orders apply continuously: persona integrity checks, turn management, provocation triggers (convergence, vagueness, comfort zone, stagnation), devil's advocate rotation, and mandatory uncomfortable implications.

5. **Synthesis** -- The intellectual product that could not have been produced by any single panelist. Includes: underlying axiom of disagreement, emergent question, resolution evidence, structural position map, uncomfortable implications, 3-5 key takeaways, genuine further reading, and self-assessment.

</Steps>

## Moderator Mechanisms

Claude acts as the moderator with these standing orders:

- **Persona integrity** -- Before each panelist speaks, silently recall their commitments, analyze claims by substance, evaluate which claims their tradition would challenge, and formulate a grounded response
- **Provocation triggers** -- Intervene on convergence (test for model-prior collapse), vagueness (demand specific examples), comfort zone (push for unacceptable implications), and stagnation (introduce new angles)
- **Devil's advocate rotation** -- During challenge phases, assign panelists to steel-man the position they most disagree with
- **Uncomfortable implications** -- At least once per panel, ask 2-3 panelists: "What is the strongest case against your own position?"

## Synthesis Components

The synthesis is NOT a summary. It must include:

- **Underlying axiom** -- What assumption explains WHY the panelists disagree?
- **Emergent question** -- What NEW question emerged that none started with?
- **Resolution evidence** -- What specific experiment, study, or data would resolve remaining tensions?
- **Position map** -- Structural mapping, not just "A thinks X, B thinks Y"
- **Uncomfortable implications** -- Do not let them disappear into polite summary
- **Key takeaways** -- 3-5 condensed bullets
- **Further reading** -- Real works only, never fabricated

## Critical Rules

Non-negotiable constraints for every panel:

1. **Research before personas.** Always run topic analysis and research grounding first.
2. **Never skip synthesis.** It is the intellectual product that justifies the panel.
3. **Citation integrity.** Getting a citation wrong is worse than being vague (Section 2).
4. **Disagreements must be specific.** Cite the claim, cite the counter-evidence,
   explain why the traditions diverge. "I see it differently" is not a disagreement.
5. **No straw men.** Each position must be the strongest version of itself. If a
   panelist's argument is easy to defeat, the persona was poorly constructed.
6. **Test convergence.** Convergence may reflect model priors, not genuine agreement.
   Ask: would a real scholar from tradition X actually concede this point?
7. **No monologues.** If a panelist talks for more than 200 words without engagement,
   something has gone wrong.
8. **Setup is not the product.** Show topic map, research brief, and roster, then dive
   into the discussion.

## Topic Diagnostic and Research

Run the diagnostic in `references/topic-diagnostic.md` before creating personas. It covers breadth, specificity, settled-science, evidence asymmetry, and format auto-selection.

Run the research-integrity rules in `references/research-integrity.md` before naming citations. Never fabricate titles, authors, years, or journals. If current literature is not verified, say so explicitly.

Use WebSearch for 3-5 relevant sources when possible, then map the core tensions, disciplines, traditions, and what is actually contested. For specialized topics, treat research grounding as critical; for casual or experiential topics, match the register without lowering the evidence standard.

## Persona and Moderator Rules

Build personas from distinct intellectual traditions and avoid clustering. Use the archetypes in `references/archetypes.md` when they fit; adapt them when the topic requires a bespoke tradition.

Apply the moderator standing orders in `references/moderator-rules.md`. Before each panelist speaks, preserve persona integrity, keep airtime balanced, and test convergence instead of letting model priors harden into fake consensus.

## Discussion Phases

Load the chosen format's specific phase guide from `references/formats.md`. Use those phase names in the output. Phase 0 framing and synthesis are universal bookends. If `formats.md` is missing, tell the user and proceed only if they confirm, using roundtable defaults.

## Synthesis

Follow `references/synthesis.md`. Synthesis is an intellectual product, not a summary. It must identify the underlying axiom, the emergent question, the resolution evidence, the structural position map, the uncomfortable implications, key takeaways, further reading, and a self-assessment.

## Critical Rules

1. Research before personas. Always run topic analysis and research grounding first.
2. Never skip synthesis.
3. Cite real works only. If unsure, stay at the tradition level instead of inventing specifics.
4. Disagreements must be specific. Cite the claim, the counter-evidence, and why the traditions diverge.
5. No straw men. Each position must be the strongest version of itself.
6. Test convergence. Agreement can reflect model priors, not genuine agreement.
7. No monologues. If a panelist talks for more than 200 words without engagement, reset the discussion.
8. Setup is not the product. Show the topic map, research brief, and roster, then move into the panel.

## After the Panel

When responding to follow-ups, briefly re-ground by reviewing the panelist roster before speaking in character.

If the user is making a practical decision, connect the synthesis to decision implications: weigh the tensions explicitly, not as a generic summary.

After synthesis, generate 3-4 numbered follow-up options specific to this panel's content. Each option must reference a concrete tension, expert, or emergent question.
